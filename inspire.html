Spider-Man

What makes a hero?

Spider-Man is considered a superhero for several reasons. His unique combination of superpowers, strong moral code, and compelling orgin story. fter being bitten by a radioactive spider, he gains super strength, agility, and a "spider-sense." Guided by "With great power comes great responsibility," he uses his abilities to protect others.

Superman, born on Krypton and raised as Clark Kent on Earth, possesses incredible powers like super strength, flight, and heat vision. His strong moral compass, shaped by his adoptive parents, drives him to use his abilities to protect humanity and fight evil. As both a superhero and a journalist, he balances his dual identity to uphold justice and inspire hope.
